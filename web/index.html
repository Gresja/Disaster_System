What is happening here?

Imagine a real disaster strikes and 5 different areas are hit at the same time. Each area starts reporting emergencies. A team of 4 responders work in parallel (at the same time) to handle these emergencies. But there are limited resources (only 8 ambulances, 5 shelters, etc.) — so when a resource runs out, a responder must wait until another one finishes and frees it up. This is the core idea of parallel programming: multiple things happening simultaneously, sharing resources safely.
